Navient Lawsuits in the DMV Area over the Last 4 Years

By: Aanisah Husain

Story sourced from this Student Loan Planner article

All data sourced from consumerfinance.gov




Navient Corporation, one of the largest federal student loan servicers, according to Student Loan Planner, has faced a flurry of lawsuits in recent years. Student Loan Planner says the lawsuits come after hundreds of thousands of recipients of loans, with Navient being their provider, complained of being taken advantage of such as failing to apply the loan payments correctly, forcing some borrowers toward multiple forebearances, being deceptive and reporting loans incorrectly to credit bureaus. Earlier this year, in 2022, Navient, though continuing to claim their innocence, has reached a settlement of $1.85 billion in relief to those who have been impacted.


Below are graphics displaying a few trends noticed over the last four years as Navient faced these lawsuits.



This first chart analyzes which area in the DMV (DC, Maryland and Virginia) filed the most complaints about Navient over the course of the last four years. It can be seen that a majority of the complaints came from Virginia, with Maryland in second and hardly any in the District of Columbia (possibly due to the area's size).

This next chart analyzes how many of the complaints involved federal student loan servicing and how many involved private student loans. This final graph analyzes the trend as to how many complaints have been received in D.C., Maryland and Virginia over the course of the last four years. The data shows that we see an influx of complaints during the pandemic, with each region in the DMV filing the most complaints in 2020, the year in which the pandemic started. There is a decrease in the number of complaints filed in 2021, as people settle into the pandemic, but we see a rise in complaints again in 2022.
